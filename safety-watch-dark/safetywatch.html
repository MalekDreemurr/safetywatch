Limitations & Future Work

⊗ Current Limitations

Performance degrades in low-light conditions or high-contrast scenarios
Camera Angle Dependency
Face recognition accuracy drops with extreme angles (>45°)
False Positives
Occasional misclassification of rapid movements as violent behavior
Single Camera Support
Currently optimized for one camera stream at a time

Dataset & Training Process

📦

Real-World Safety Scenarios Dataset

Our models are trained on comprehensive datasets containing thousands of labeled images and videos representing real workplace scenarios, both normal and unsafe behaviors.

🔗

Dataset Composition

🎥 Violence Detection Dataset
✓
SCVD: Surveillance Camera Violence Dataset
✓
RLVS: Real Life Violence Situations Dataset
✓
RWF2000: Real-World Fighting Dataset
✓
Total: 5,000+ labeled video clips
👤 Face Recognition Dataset
✓
Custom datasets created by each manager/admin
✓
Multiple face angles and lighting conditions per employee
✓
Minimum 10-20 images per person for optimal accuracy
Data Labeling Strategy
Normal Behavior: Walking, sitting, working, talking
Unsafe Behavior: Fighting, pushing, aggressive gestures
📊

Preprocessing Pipeline

1
FaceNet with VGGFace2: Pretrained model extracts 128-dimensional face embeddings (feature vectors)
2
Video Frame Extraction: Extract frames at 15-30 FPS for temporal analysis
3
Resizing & Normalization: All images resized to 224×224, pixel values normalized to [0,1]
4
Data Augmentation: Random rotation (±15°), horizontal flip, brightness adjustment
5
Standardization: Mean centering and standard deviation scaling
📈

Training Configuration

Methodology
Transfer Learning: ImageNet pretrained weights
Optimizer: Adam (lr=0.001)
Epochs: 50 with early stopping
Batch Size: 32 samples
Validation
Split: 80% train / 20% validation
Cross-Validation: 5-fold CV
Metrics: Accuracy, F1, Precision, Recall
Loss: Binary cross-entropy
90%+ Final Accuracy
0.92 F1 Score
<5% False Positive Rate
